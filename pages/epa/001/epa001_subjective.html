Pronouns are important in the visit and in charting - pronouns reflect how we perceive someone’s gender. Trans 101: If someone is a transgender man, that means he was probably assigned female at birth and may have a vulva, uterus, cervix, ovaries, and breasts - all of these must be examined as for any cisgender female patient.
